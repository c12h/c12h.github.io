Background: Slitherine Software released a tabletop system named Field of Glory for Ancient and Medieval wargaming with miniatures in 2008. In 2009, Slitherine released a TBT Windows game with the same name; it eventually got 9 DLC. Field of Glory II (W/-/Gold, still TBT) came out in 2017; one Steam reviewer says it is much better than the 2009 game. The base game covers Rome from 280 BC to 25 BC; it has 5 DLC ranging from 681 BC to 1040 AD. In contrast Field of Glory: Empires (see above) is a grand strategy game from 2019; in fact, it allows you to export battles to FoG II and load the results back in.

Conclusion: Paid AU$20 to get Field of Glory: Empires, which has never been cheaper than AU$34, plus some quality FRGPS to trade or give away.
